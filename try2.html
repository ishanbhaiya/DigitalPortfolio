Interdisciplinary Projects & Research

1) AI-based Credit Risk Modeling

Objectives: Build an explainable model to predict borrower default probability.
Methodologies:
  • Data: synthetic & anonymized financial behaviour features; feature engineering for credit history.
  • Models: logistic regression, random forest, calibration; cross-validation.
  • Explainability: SHAP values to interpret feature influence.
Outcomes & innovations:
  • Achieved balanced accuracy with threshold tuning and calibration plots.
  • Introduced simple risk-banding (Low/Medium/High) for explainable reporting.
Challenges & solutions:
  • Imbalanced labels — solved using stratified sampling & class weights.
  • Data leakage risk — strict temporal splitting and pipeline encapsulation.

Outcomes:
  • Identified firms with persistent margin advantage and underlying drivers (scale, distribution).
  • Provided practical recommendations for valuation inputs (terminal growth, margin normalization).
Artifacts:

Global Awareness & Ethical Considerations

Case study — AI & Credit Decisions

Scenario

During credit-model development, we discovered features highly correlated with socio-economic status. Using them naively risked biased outcomes against underrepresented groups.

Ethical analysis & actions

  • Performed bias diagnostics (disparate impact, fairness metrics).
  • Removed/modified proxy variables and introduced fairness constraints in model training.
  • Documented limitations and recommended human-in-the-loop review for edge-cases.

Reflection

I believe responsible ML requires continuous monitoring, clear documentation of assumptions, and conservative deployment policies to avoid harm.

Global perspective

Considerations

  • Understanding regulatory differences across markets (data privacy, KYC).
  • Designing models that are adaptable and culturally aware when used in multiple regions.
